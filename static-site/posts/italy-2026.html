Italy — Spring 2026

April 2026

Rome was obligatory, but Oriolo, Orvieto, and Nani were the actual trip. Small towns where we were the only tourists, a campfire in the middle of a flower field, chess on the edge of a cliff wall, and taking my friend to her first metal show. Italy turned out to be a lot more interesting once we stopped looking at the tourist map.

Orvieto

A hill town built on top of a volcanic cliff in Umbria. We played chess on the wall ledge with a 100-metre drop behind the board. The view made it hard to focus on the game.

Nani

Ancient ruins, terraced gardens, and green hills in every direction. The kind of place where you end up staying longer than planned because there's always one more corner to turn.

Oriolo

First proper camping trip — tent in a flower field, fire going before dark. Paolo lives here and walks his cat on a lead down the country lane every morning. That felt like a good sign.

Roma

Obligatory. The Colosseum is as big as you'd expect. The Trevi Fountain is more crowded than you'd hope. The prosciutto and white wine made up for it. We left after a day.
