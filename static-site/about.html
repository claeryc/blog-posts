About

The person behind the blog.

I'm Eric — also 思烨 (Sīyè). I took a gap year to learn Mandarin properly, which meant moving to Nanjing, attending university classes, and living in a dorm with a bathroom I'd rather not describe. It worked, mostly.

Before that: a solo trip to Thailand where I ended up spending a week with a group of Chinese travellers I met at a jazz bar. That trip is what started all of this.

The gap year is done now and I'm back at work, but this site remains — part travel log, part language journal, part dumping ground for whatever else I end up doing.

✦

The three main threads on this site are the Mandarin study project (classes, progress, the whole experience of studying at a Chinese university), a travel section covering the places I've been, and a general blog for everything else — including a post about AI that I'm not fully sure I stand behind anymore.

The /now page has a running log of what I'm actually up to, newest first. That's probably the best place to start if you want the current picture.
