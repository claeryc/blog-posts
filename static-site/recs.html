Recs

Things I've found genuinely useful — books, tools, language resources.

📚 Books

  • The Secret History — Donna Tartt. Read it on the train between cities in China. Probably the best time and place for it.
  • Thinking, Fast and Slow — Daniel Kahneman. Genuinely changed how I notice my own reasoning. Dense but worth it.
  • Pachinko — Min Jin Lee. Picked it up while studying in Nanjing. The themes hit differently when you're living in Asia.

🈶 Mandarin learning

  • Pleco — The dictionary app. Nothing else comes close for Chinese learners. The flashcard system is also solid once you start building a deck.
  • Anki — Spaced repetition flashcards. Ugly, but nothing beats it for building vocab retention over time.
  • Shadowing — Not an app, just a method: listen to native audio and repeat it simultaneously. Felt ridiculous at first. Genuinely the fastest thing I found for improving tones.

🛠 Tools

  • Obsidian — Where I kept my notes during the gap year. Local files, fast, and the graph view is genuinely useful once you have enough notes.
  • nownownow.com — The idea behind my /now page. A simple concept: a public page saying what you're actually up to right now.

More to come as I think of things worth recommending.
