My Learning Story

Did you ever wanted to challenge your view on culture and society, or wanted to step outside your comfort zone? Deciding to learn Chinese did just that for me, let me explain how and what I mean by this.

By learning a new language, you not only acquire the skill to use it for reading or speaking, but you will also be more immersed in the culture in which it is spoken. Being aware of it can be quite crucial, since it can make the entire process more fun and fulfilling.

The main factor that accounts for being consistent with learning and turning it into a habit, is motivation. There is not however, this one blueprint one has to follow, to reach it. It heavily relies on personal circumstances and preferences. One has to ask themselves, whats the reason of wanting to learn this language?

  • is it just pure boredom?
  • do I want to travel to another country?
  • do I have friends or family who speak that language?
  • do I want to challenge my worldview and be open to new experiences?

The last factor was my personal reason. Depending on what your reason might be, can significantly impact your motivation and time required to study and reach your personally set goals.

Below, I'll lay out how exactly I went about how I started, what resources I used, what my progress looks like, and what I have learned throughout my studies, starting from using simple apps, all the way to living in China and studying in one of the most populated cities.
